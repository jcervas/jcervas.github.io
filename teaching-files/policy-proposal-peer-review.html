Peer Review Referee Report — Policy Proposal

Assignment: Policy Proposals (Due December 5). Target length of proposals: 2–4 single‑spaced pages (visualizations excluded). Reviewers: read two peers’ proposals and complete this report. Your feedback is graded for thoughtfulness, specificity, and constructiveness.

1. Summary of the Proposal

Briefly (3–5 sentences) describe the policy problem, the recommended policy, and the core evidence/historical analysis used.

2. Strengths

List at least three notable strengths (e.g., clear writing, appropriate data, strong historical context, originality, good visualizations).

3. Areas for Improvement

Identify at least three specific, actionable improvements. Pair each issue with a constructive suggestion.

4. Evidence and Historical Analysis

  • Is the data relevant, sufficient, and clearly presented?
  • Is the historical context accurate and well‑integrated?
  • Are visualizations informative, labeled, and reproducible?
  • Are limitations, assumptions, and uncertainty discussed?

5. Organization and Clarity

  • Is the argument logically structured and easy to follow?
  • Are transitions, headings, and signposting effective?
  • Is the writing concise and mostly error‑free?

6. Overall Assessment

Provide a brief overall verdict. Consider persuasiveness, feasibility, and policy relevance.

7. Suggested Grade

The instructor will average peer grades and may adjust final grades. If you believe your own grade should differ, you may request an evaluation before the end of the semester; note the instructor may lower grades at their discretion.

8. Confidential Comments to Instructor (Optional)

Optional remarks for the instructor that will not be shared with the author.
